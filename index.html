02 Research & Publications

My primary research develops scalable low-rank variational inference methods (W ≈ ABT) for Bayesian neural networks, with provable guarantees and an emphasis on parameter efficiency. I also study whether these structures let networks automatically adapt their complexity to the data generating process (Occam's Razor).

ICML 2026
Seoul, July 2026
Singular Bayesian Neural Networks Accepted
Mame Diarra Toure, David A. Stephens
International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026
PDF / arXiv / Project page

A low-rank variational inference framework for Bayesian neural networks that parameterizes weights as W = ABT, reducing parameter complexity from O(mn) to O((m+n)r). This induces a posterior singular with respect to the Lebesgue measure, capturing structured weight correlations through shared latent factors. We derive PAC-Bayes generalization bounds and prove loss bounds via the Eckart-Young-Mirsky theorem. Empirically, the method matches 5-member Deep Ensembles with up to 33× fewer parameters and substantially improves out-of-distribution detection across MLPs, LSTMs, and Transformers.

Preprint
2026
Not Just How Much, But Where: Decomposing Epistemic Uncertainty into Per-Class Contributions
Mame Diarra Toure, David A. Stephens
arXiv:2602.21160 (under review), 2026
PDF / arXiv

We decompose mutual information into a per-class uncertainty vector Ck(x) = σk² / (2μk), letting safety-critical classifiers distinguish where a model is uncertain, not just how much. Applied to diabetic retinopathy grading, the critical-class score reduces selective risk by 34.7% over mutual information and 56.2% over variance baselines.

When Does a Low-Rank Bayesian Neural Network Certify Its Deterministic Center? Accepted
Mame Diarra Toure, David A. Stephens
SPIGM Workshop (Structured Probabilistic Inference & Generative Modeling), ICML 2026
PDF

We study when a structured low-rank Gaussian variational posterior can certify a deterministic predictor in a Bayesian neural network with factorized layers Wi = AiBiT. The same model yields three natural certification targets; this paper focuses on the deterministic-center route.

Hydro-Québec (IREQ)
May to Dec 2026
Research Scientist

Adapting the Singular Bayesian Neural Network framework to self-supervised pretraining of Transformer encoders on large-scale industrial time series. The host pipeline comprises ~8,000 specialist encoders pretrained on hydroelectric sensor signals; each encoder is simultaneously compressed via learned low-rank weight factorizations and equipped with calibrated epistemic uncertainty through variational inference, enabling principled encoder selection for load forecasting and fault detection.
